3.Start to analyze

  1. Click Download.

  2. Please carefully check Description and Unit.

    Note

    In bioinformatics data processing, raw RNA-seq expression data or proteomics mass spectrometry data typically exhibit a wide dynamic range and significant skewness. To make the distribution closer to normality, it is common practice to apply a log₂ transformation to the expression values. Log₂ transformation compresses extreme large values, reduces skewness, and stabilizes variance across the dataset. This adjustment facilitates subsequent statistical analyses, such as t-tests and linear regression, which generally assume normality and homoscedasticity, thereby enhancing the reliability and interpretability of the results. Especially in proteomics, where mass spectrometry signals inherently span several orders of magnitude and follow an exponential-like distribution, log₂ transformation has become a standard preprocessing step.

4. Be Careful!

  • Ensure data approximates a normal distribution before applying parametric tests (consider log₂ transformation or Z-score if needed).

  • For small sample sizes, use non-parametric tests like the Wilcoxon test.

  • Always perform multiple testing correction (e.g., FDR) when analyzing thousands of features.

  • Remember: Statistical significance does not imply biological relevance; consider fold-change thresholds.

  • Avoid p-hacking; define your analysis plan before examining the results.

  • Take care of COX analyze, ask GPT about the information of it.
